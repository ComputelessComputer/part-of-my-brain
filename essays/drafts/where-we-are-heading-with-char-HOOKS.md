# Three opening hooks — pick one, I'll expand

These are draft openers only. ~150 words each. Picking the angle now saves a 2000-word rewrite later.

---

## Option A — Comms surface = task surface

I've been talking to founders and operators about how they actually run their day, and a pattern keeps showing up: **the surface where the conversation happens is the surface where the work lives.**

Paul keeps every gitdot launch in one Quip doc — strategy, eng checklist, bugs, raw user feedback, stacked vertically. Tim's CoS workflow runs on `task.md` + Claude Code, one file, lines added and killed as work moves. Ben's company runs on `#brain-<person>` Slack channels — one agent thread per teammate, persistent context across weeks. Tom uses one Slack channel routed over Notion, Apollo, Attio, Sheets, and a custom Python pipeline.

Different substrates. Same shape. None of them open a separate todo app.

That's the thing I keep coming back to when I think about what Char actually is.

---

## Option B — Ranking, not throughput

Most task apps are solving the wrong problem.

They're built for capture — get every thought in, sort it later, never lose anything. The pitch is "stop forgetting." But the people I watch get the most done don't have a capture problem. They have a ranking problem.

Paul caps his day at three todos. Andrew's tasks are boolean — done or not — and most sit untouched for months on purpose. Mahyad sorts everything into three buckets and refuses to flatten them into one list. None of them are trying to do more. They're trying to do less, better.

Sprint boards, kanbans, GTD systems — most of them are busywork theater. They optimize for *more cards moved*. The real bottleneck is almost always a small number of hard decisions being avoided.

That's where Char should sit.

---

## Option C — Where we're heading (follow-up to wedge-of-char)

Two months ago I wrote that Char is the daily note. That's still true, but it was only half the answer.

The other half came from talking to ~13 founders and operators in May about how they actually run end-to-end tasks. The cleanest finding is also the one I didn't expect: the surface where the conversation happens IS the surface where work gets tracked. Quip doc, Slack thread, `task.md` file, email chain — five substrates, same structural pattern. None of them open a separate todo app.

That sharpens what Char is: not a notepad, not a task manager, not a meeting notetaker. A **context aggregator and co-working surface**. The form factor — daily note — is what holds the context. The chat — Cortana, Claude Code, your CoS — is what does the work.

Two surfaces, one product.

## How they're different

| Angle | Lead with | Lands on | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | The pattern (5 people, 5 surfaces, same shape) | Char belongs inside chat, not as a separate app | Could read as research-y if I'm not careful |

| **C** | Sequel framing — "wedge-of-char was half the picture" | Two-surface product (daily note + chat) | Safer. Reads as update-essay, less standalone hook |

My pick: **B**. Strongest contrarian framing, sharpest John-shaped opener ("Most task apps are solving the wrong problem"), and the n=3 evidence is concrete enough to back it up. A is the safest, C is the most continuous with what you already published.
