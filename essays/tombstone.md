Everyone should think about their tombstone more often.

It's pretty different from writing a *will* — I haven't written one yet. A will is your plan for how things should play out *after* you die.

But a *tombstone* is a superset of milestones. It defines how you've lived your life — by others.

You are what others think of you. Every word you say, action you take, are remembered by someone. And frankly, these tombstones are made by the people closest to you — your family or friends.

But a tombstone is just a symbol. Let's say it's your funeral and your friends, family, and colleagues, all join and share comments. What would you like for them to say?

What if you think backwards and live by those comments that you'd like to hear?

I obviously don't think you should pretend to be someone, but words shape us more than we know. It's a powerful thing to just keep thinking about it as it eventually dictates what kind of person you are.

So if you start thinking about your tombstone — you're essentially creating a customized life plan.

So how do I want to be remembered? Probably something like this:

> Because of him, the world became full of love.

> He made humanity more thoughtful.

> STEM experienced a profound leap in innovation.

> People were able to take more risks and make bold statements in the market.
